This study supported a value-based contract enforcement protocol for an anti-psychotic treatment by identifying members with observed use of the treatment formulations across pharmacy, outpatient, and inpatient claims and then profiling the utilization patterns needed to operationalize contract measurement and validation. The goal was to assemble a contract-eligible member cohort, distinguish product/formulation use across the product portfolio, quantify how the product was being billed and supplied across settings of care, and generate the distributions and member-level counts needed to enforce contract terms, validate claim capture, and monitor real-world treatment activity for he evaluated product.
